MY PERSONAL EXPERIENCES AND ANECDOTES

When I was a kid, I was really interested in chess. I asked my grandfather if he knew how to play, and that's how I built my foundation, learning how every single piece moves. After that, I joined my city's team and had the experience of playing with some of the best players in Colombia. I acquired strategies and theoretical openings to enhance my knowledge as a chess player. I started participating in every tournament hosted in my city, which helped me develop analytical thinking and improve my ability to predict my opponent's moves. Even though I went through a losing streak, I joined my school's chess team and faced really smart players from other schools. Thanks to my effort, I reached the finals in the tournament. I was losing the game, but my opponent made a mistake and ended up stalemating me, which resulted in a draw. After that the referee told us to play a blitz game (3min + 2) and finally I got the win, making him suffer and turning out in a huge checkmate, with 2 rooks and my knight. That's how I started winning the next tournaments and receiving cups and medals. Thanks to my school's teacher I enhaced my movements in chess and winning the first place 3 times in a row.
